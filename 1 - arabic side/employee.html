Employee Relations
Working Hours +
  1. Working hours
    1. 1.1. The total normal working hours shall not exceed Forty Eight hours (48 hours) per week.
    2. 1.2. On project / sites, employees may also be required to work on weekends.
  3. General
    1. 2.1. The actual working hours during the month of Ramadan shall not exceed Six (6) hours a day or Thirty Six (36) hours a week.
    2. 2.2. The normal working hours and Ramadan working hours shall be scheduled according to business requirements.
  5. Overtime:
    1. 3.1. Definition

      If an employee performs work for the Company as per instructions of the management that falls outside of the normal working hours.

    2. 3.2. Compliance of Labor Law

      The normal working hours and Ramadan working hours shall be scheduled according to business requirements.

    3. 3.3. Approval

      All overtime has to be applied for and approved from department manager. Any claim for compensation of overtime not approved in advance will be considered null and void.

    4. 3.4. Compensation for overtime
      • Overtime worked during normal working days (Saturday - Thursday) will be paid at the rate of 1.5 times the normal hourly rate of the employee.
      • Overtime worked on weekends (Friday) and Public Holidays will be paid at the rate of 1.5 times the normal hourly rate of the employee.
      • The hourly rate will be calculated as follows:
        Annual Basic Salary = Monthly basic pay X 12
        Daily Pay = Annual / 365
        Hourly Pay = Daily / Working hours
        Overtime Hourly Pay= 1.5 X Hourly Pay
        Example: Basic salary 1000 Riyals
        Annual = 12,000
        Daily = 12,000/365 = 32.88
        Hourly= 32.88/8 = 4.11
        Overtime Hourly= 4.11 X 1.5 = 6.165
      • All overtimes should be pre-approved in writing and should not exceed 4 hours per day and the total amount per month should not exceed 50% of the basic pay.
      • All additional leave days granted in lieu of overtime payment must be used by the end of the calendar year otherwise they will be forfeit.
Leaves +
  1. Leave Salary (Salary Advance)
    • Leave salary is paid in advance if the leave period exceeds 15 calendar days. The period includes Holidays that fall before and after the leave period.
    • Leave Salary includes the Monthly Basic Salary and all allowances paid monthly during employee service.
  2. Annual Leave
  4. The following rules apply:
    • Employees will be entitled to a paid leave starting from 21 up to 30 working days, either yearly or every two years, depending on their employment contracts.
    • Annual leave will be in balance starting from 1st of January and can be used any time during the year.
    • In case an employee resigns after leave usage the balance will be deducted from the final settlement.
    • All employees must take their annual leave every year.
    • If, due to business or personal reasons, an employee has not been able to take his/her full Leave entitlement for the year, up to 10 working days can be transferred (no encashment) to the next year, but should be utilized by the end of the first quarter. If not utilized then these days will be cancelled.
    • All leave balances over 10 days at the end of December will be cancelled and written off. New employees are excluded from this rule in their first year of employment if their start date is later than 1st August.
    • No cash payment will be made against the balance leave, except in case of termination of services.
    • An application for annual leave should be submitted at least one month prior to the planned leave.
    • Return Advice must be submitted to HR Services within 3 working days after the employee’s return.
    • If the employee is unable to return on schedule in case of emergency, he/she should inform his/her manager and seek approval for extension, and then should be attested from HR.
  6. Sick Leave

  7. An employee on sick leave is entitled to receive his salary for the said period as follows:

    1. Full salary payment up to the first thirty (<=30) days.
    2. 75% of salary payment for the next sixty (31-90) days.
    3. The period beyond ninety (>90) days will be treated as unpaid leave until the employee resumes his/her duties.

    A Medical Certificate from a doctor/hospital approved by the Company shall support a sick leave. If the Medical Certificate is not provided to the company within 3 working days of absence, the department will reject the sick leave.

    An employee forced to extend his/her leave due to sickness while abroad should immediately inform his/her manager who is required to inform HR Services. After return, the employee must present a Medical Certificate from an accredited hospital. Failure to do so will be regarded as “Absent without Permission”, leading to termination.

  8. Maternity Leave

  9. Female employees are entitled to a pre maternity leave of 4 weeks before delivery day, and a leave of 6 weeks after delivery, with full pay after completing at least nine (9) months of service.

    1. Maternity leave salary is payable in advance.
    2. Female employees who resume work after the Maternity leave are entitled for one hour additional break per day up to the first two years after delivery.
  10. Iddah Leave

  11. Female employees are entitled to iddah leave of 15 working days in the event of divorce/death of her husband. Official documents are required.

  12. Hajj Leave

  13. As per the Saudi Labor Law; the employee will be entitled for a one time paid leave during his/her service to perform Hajj after completion of 2 years, number of days will be from 10 -15 calendar days including Hajj holidays’ days.

  14. Compassionate Leave

  15. In the event of the death of an employee’s father, mother, brother, sister, spouse/husband or child, he/she will be entitled to paid compassionate leave of up to three (3) Calendar days.

  16. Marriage and Child Birth Leave

  17. Employees are entitled to three (3) business days leave with full pay on the occasion of their marriage and one day paid leave for birth of a child.

  18. Exam Leave

  19. Exam Leave is a paid leave granted to employees on the actual days they sit for exams. The employee will need to submit the official exam results and exam timetable in order to confirm their attendance; otherwise it will be deducted from their annual leave. This is granted at the discretion of HR approval.

  20. Unpaid Leave

  21. An employee may be granted a maximum period of 15 calendar days unpaid leave per year and must be approved by his Department Head/VP, CHRO and the President.

Public Holidays +

The company will be closed during Eid-al-Fitr and Eid-al-Adha holidays, plus on 23rd September (Saudi National Day) in accordance with the announcements made every year with Company Circulars.

Employee Disciplinary Rules +

Saudi labor rules and regulations (Regulations) provide a range of disciplinary measures that the employer should consider before finally terminating an employee’s contract. The key procedures afforded to employers under the Regulations are as follows:

  • The employer should issue first a verbal warning to the employee followed by a written warning if the employee persists on committing the wrongdoing.
  • A fine may be imposed in the form of salary deductions. However, the fine should not exceed five day salary per month for a single offence.
  • The employer is also permitted, under the Regulations, to cancel or defer the employee’s allowances for a period not exceeding one year.
  • In addition to periodic allowances, the employer can defer expected promotions of the disciplined employee for a period of maximum one year.
  • Under the Regulations, temporary suspension is another disciplinary option available to the employer. During the suspension period, the employee is not entitled to a salary. However, the suspension without salary should not last for more than five days.
  • Final termination with or without end-of-service benefits is permitted if the employee commits a deliberate offence against life, property, honor or honesty.
Domestic Relocation +

All new expatriate employees on joining Alturki Holding from another country or another city will be provided with accommodation facility for an initial period of 15 days until suitable residential accommodation is obtained. All personal expenses including food will be at the employee’s personal account. The type of accommodation and/or the amount in lieu are as mentioned in Benefits Table, Item No. B9 & B13 of Chapter 5 - Total Reward Policy.

CXOs/VPs/Directors upon joining Alturki Holding from another country or another city will be entitled to rent a car for an initial period of 30 days in addition to the accommodation. Such car will be rented by the Company and assigned to the employee. The type of car or cost related will be as mentioned in the Chapter 4 – Business Travel Policy under Car Travel.

Guidelines

In the case of Full Time Employees (FTE) transferred from different regions within the Kingdom or from abroad, Alturki Holding will support them for relocation expenses as follows:

  • Transferred employees within the Kingdom of Saudi Arabia or from abroad are eligible for one month basic salary OR SAR. 15,000 (whichever is higher) at the time of transfer (that should include shipping/moving costs) subject to invoice.
  • Once selected, candidates from different regions are entitled to a Company-paid air ticket from nearest airport to the workplace.
