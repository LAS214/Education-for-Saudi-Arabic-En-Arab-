TOTAL REWARDS
Policy Statement +

Our compensation programs directly link payment to business and individual performance. Alturki Holding’s programs are designed to be:

1.1. Performance-driven: Alturki Holding rewards performance by providing faster compensation growth for high-performing employees, as determined through Alturki Holding Performance Policy.

1.2. Individually Differentiated: Alturki Holding provides opportunities for above-market compensation and promotion on the basis of demonstrated individual results and behaviors.

1.3. Aligned with business goals: In order to drive business success, Alturki Holding uses compensation to reinforce goal alignment between individuals and business objectives.

1.4. Market competitive: Alturki Holding is committed to providing compensation opportunities that are competitive with those of other companies with which we compete for talent.

1.5. Fair and consistent: Alturki Holding’s compensation program is designed in such a way that compensation opportunities for similar positions are internally consistent across all subsidiaries, equitable and non-discriminatory.

1.6. Open and transparent: Alturki Holding openly communicates how pay programs work and how individual compensation is determined.

1.7. Globally oriented: Alturki Holding establishes global compensation policies and practices that are as sensitive to local differences as possible.

1.8. Compliant with local laws: Alturki Holding follows the Saudi Labor Law.

2.3. Salary Market Survey

The purpose of conducting Salary Market Survey is to provide the Company with data in order to set salaries which are relevant to the market. To ensure that the data is relevant to Alturki Holding, the Company identifies markets where at least 50% of the jobs are similar to those at Alturki Holding and therefore would offer a more relevant basis for comparison. The resulting market data allows the Company to make informed decisions on compensation programs such as pricing jobs, analyzing pay trends, identifying pay practices and establishing a salary structure.

2.4. Salary Planning

For every compensation program, the first year cost and any ongoing costs associated with the program must be accurately calculated. To determine the cost of these programs, specific information is required on three key elements:

  • The size of the average increase when looking at structure adjustments and amounts of actual increase when looking at market adjustments;
  • The number of employees receiving increases, including the frequency of the increases; and
  • The timing which the increases become effective.

2.5. Financial Budgeting

2.6. Pay Review

The objectives of pay review are to reward employees in accordance with their performance over the past year, and to review their compensation against the market to ensure that they are rewarded competitively.

2.7. Eligibility for Pay Review

It is not mandatory for Alturki Holding to conduct pay review for individual employees. Generally, employees who have successfully completed at least one (1) year on 31 December of any given year may be considered for pay review. Employees who are not entitled for a pay review or have submitted their resignation letters prior to the formal notification of the pay adjustment will not be considered.

2.8. Review Process

The pay review/salary adjustments will normally be made effective on January 1, each year. In order to ensure that reasonable and equitable pay adjustments are made, the Human Resources Department will issue clear guidelines to all CXOs and VPs in this regard. The Human Resources Department may provide indicative adjustment rates for specific job levels or positions that require special attention based on market information sources such as the Hay Group Country reports. While the CXOs, VPs and the Heads of Department are to use the guidelines for reference, they should make adjustments in their recommendations to accommodate for specific circumstances in their Department. However, they should provide justification to the Human Resources Department for any exceptional pay increase or promotion. As recommendations for pay review and promotion are subject to final approval of the President, CXOs, VPs and Heads of Department should not make any premature commitment or disclosure to their employees.

2.9. Salary on Transfer

Employees transferring between Departments in the same level or to a different professional business line without taking up a higher level of responsibility will not receive a salary increase.

2.10. Salary on Promotion

On promotion to a higher level, an employee can expect an increase (not exceeding 20%) that is in line with the new level; alternatively, his/her CXO, VP, and Head of Department will work out a salary progression scheme in coordination with the Human Resources Department.

2.11. Salary for Temporary Appointments

The salaries to be offered for temporary appointments such as student trainees, temporary assistants, part-time employees, contracted and talent sharing do not fit into the salary structure for permanent employees. Their salaries will be determined by taking into consideration the prevailing market rates and Company budget.

2.12. Confidentiality of Salary Information

Individual salary information is strictly confidential. Only the individual employee, his CXO, VP, Head of Department and employees who process salary and benefits will have access to the employee's salary information. Employees should not disclose their salaries to persons other than their Head of Department or his/her designated officer. Employees who have access to salary information in the course of their duties must handle the information with extreme care to ensure confidentiality.

Adjustment Budget FAQs +

What is a market adjustment?

Market adjustments are used to align pay to internal factors and/or external market. Adjustments should be considered for employees that are high performers whose compensation is low compared to the market range.

When and why do we provide an adjustment?

Adjustments are provided when an employee’s salary is low relative to internal equity or market data.

  1. Market adjustments should not be used to compensate for below average merit increases and should not be expected or given regularly.
  2. Market adjustments are not warranted for all employees below the range.

What types of salary increases can be planned? Can I plan an adjustment?

There are three types of salary increases that can be planned:

  1. Merit: reflects an employee's performance during the prior year;
  2. Adjustment: given when an employee's salary is significantly low relative to the market;

What is the role of individual performance in determining a merit increase?

There are 3 key factors that you should consider when determining an employee's merit increase:

  1. Their results and behaviors in the prior year (performance review score);
  2. Their salary relative to the market reference points; and
  3. The Budget allocated for salary increase.
Employee Loan Policy +
Purpose

The purpose of this policy is to identify circumstances where a Hardship Loan/Personal Loan can be granted to an employee.

Types of Loans
1. Hardship Loan
2. Personal Loan


1. Hardship Loan Policy

Scope

Employee Hardship Loan will only be granted where there is a serious personal need or where emergency relief is required.

Eligibility

All full time employees.

Policy
  • Regular full-time employees may request an interest-free Hardship Loan up to a maximum of three (3) months’ salary.
  • Repayments are to be deducted in accordance with the loan agreement from the employees’ salaries and must be repayable within ten (10) months. Deductions should not exceed 30% of the employee’s monthly net remuneration.
  • It is the responsibility of the employee to provide proof/supporting documentation to substantiate the loan application.
  • Employees may not request a Hardship Loan while in probation period.
  • Employees can use this benefit once every two (2) years.
  • End of service benefits for the applicant should cover the Hardship Loan amount.
  • Employees should provide a guarantor who will assure that the end-of-service benefit of the guarantor is sufficient to cover the loan and the amount will be fully repaid by the guarantor in case of any default.
  • An employee seeking to apply for a Hardship Loan must obtain an application from Human Resources. Human Resources must sign off on the Hardship Loan Application based on eligibility.
  • The HR will review all submitted loan applications. All applications will be prioritized on the basis of the following criteria:
    1. Purpose of the Hardship Loan: Justified with a reasonable propose.
    2. Amount of Hardship Loan: The amount of requested Hardship Loan does not exceed three (3) months’ salary.
    3. Hardship Loan Recovery: The Hardship Loan can be recovered from employee EOSB in the event where employee’s service with the company is discontinued.
    4. Term of Hardship Loan: Payroll deductions amount up to 30% of monthly remuneration per pay period with employee acceptance.
    5. Employee’s Outstanding Indebtedness: Employee will not be eligible for a Hardship Loan in the case of outstanding debt with the company.
  • A Hardship Loan Agreement between the “Company” and the “Employee” must be signed after obtaining required approvals.
  • Payment will not be made until the Hardship Loan Agreement has been signed and returned to the H.R. Department.
  • All Hardship Loan Agreements and supporting documentation will be filed on the employee’s personal file.
  • Human Resources will ensure that all Hardship Loans repayments MUST be processed on the payroll system.
  • Additional Hardship Loans: An employee may not apply for an additional Hardship Loan until there is a zero balance on prior loans.
  • The employee Hardship Loan Program is designed to help those employees who have exhibited good work practices and who are having financial hardships as defined above.


2. Personal Loan Policy

Scope

To support employees’ financial needs, the company encourages the employees to obtain Personal Loan through a bank where agreements between the Company and local banks are in place. Personal Loans are typically designed to support employees’ general purpose.

Eligibility

All full time employees.

Policy
  • Regular full-time employees may request for a Personal Loan through a bank where an agreement is in place.
  • An employee seeking to apply for a Personal Loan must obtain a letter from Human Resources to be submitted to the bank.
  • Human Resources will ensure that all Personal Loans are registered in payroll for salary transfer proposes.

The company is currently working on Personal Loan agreements with several banks and will insure obtaining lowest interest rates for the best interest of employees.

Market Based Pay

The following market definitions were used to create the external market analysis:

Pay Elements

  1. Basic Salary: The basic monthly cash amount paid for work performed before any paid allowances.
  2. Base Salary: Basic Salary plus Housing Allowance and TransportationAllowance.

Market Lines

  1. D9 Market Line: The Upper Decile (90th percentile) which divides the top 10% from the remaining 90% of companies.
  2. Q3 Market Line (Alturki Position): The Upper Quartile (75th percentile) which divides the top 25% from the remaining 75% of companies.
  3. Median Market Line: The Median (50th percentile) which divides the practices into two equal 50% groups, 50% are below and 50% are above.
  4. Q1 Market Line: The Lower Quartile (25th percentile) which divides the lowest 25% from the remaining 75% of companies.
  5. D1 Market Line: The Lower Decile (10th percentile) which divides the bottom 10% from the remaining 90% of companies.
